Pictographs

A pictograph is the representation of data using images. Pictographs represent the frequency of data while using symbols or images that are relevant to the data. This is one of the simplest ways to represent statistical data and reading a pictograph is made extremely easy as well.

A pictograph is also known as pictogram, pictorial chart, picture graph and many more.

How to make a Pictograph :

We must represent how many TV sets have been sold in the last few years via a pictograph.

Step 1 : Collect Data: First step is obviously collecting the dot of the category you wary to represent.

Step 2 : Pick your symbol: Pick a symbol ar picture that accu ately represents your data.

step 3 : Assign a Key: Sometimes the frequency of the data I too high. Then one symbol cannot represent one frequency. You must set a numerical value that one symbol will represent. This numerical value must be written along with the pictograph. Example am symbol of a TV represents 500 TV sets. This is the key of the pictograph.

Step 4 : Draw the pictograph: Final step is drawing your pictograph. Draw the two columns that represent the category and the data. Then draw the actual symbols that represent the frequencies. Remember that the symbols can be drawn as fractions as well if the frequency is not a whole number.

Step 5 : Review your Data: Finally, review your pictograph and make sure it correctly represents the information that you wanted to relay. DOM forget to check the labelling of your graph.
